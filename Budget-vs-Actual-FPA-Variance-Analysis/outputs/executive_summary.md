# Executive Summary - Budget vs. Actual Variance Analysis

## Business view

This analysis turns budget and actual expense data into a monthly FP&A variance package. It helps leadership understand whether spending is tracking to plan, which accounts explain the variance, and where forecast assumptions should be updated.

## Questions answered

- Are total actual expenses materially above or below budget?
- Which expense categories explain the largest favorable or unfavorable variances?
- Which variances are large enough to require management commentary or forecast updates?

## Portfolio snapshot

- Total budget: 1,762,200
- Total actuals: 1,718,180
- Net variance: -44,020, or -2.5 percent

## Largest variance drivers

- FP&A Portfolio / Development Costs: -25,565, or -1.9 percent favorable.
- FP&A Portfolio / Training Cost: -18,000, or -20.9 percent favorable.
- FP&A Portfolio / Operational Costs: 9,082, or 5.5 percent unfavorable.
- FP&A Portfolio / Travelling Cost: -6,700, or -14.0 percent favorable.
- FP&A Portfolio / Marketing Costs: -5,324, or -7.1 percent favorable.

## Recommended management actions

- Review material unfavorable variances with the accountable cost owner before the next monthly review.
- Separate timing variances from true run-rate changes before updating the rolling forecast.
- Add recurring variance drivers to the forecast assumptions log.
